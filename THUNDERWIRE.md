# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
# PHASE 1: SIGNAL INTELLIGENCE
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Signal Intelligence is not research. Research summarizes what exists. Signal Intelligence detects what's about to exist — topics in their rising phase before saturation, audience language mined verbatim from primary sources, content gaps competitors haven't owned.

## 1.1 — Trend Velocity Classification

Before any content decision, classify the topic's velocity:

```
🚀 RISING    → Mentions/searches growing >20% week-over-week. Maximum opportunity.
               Move immediately. Content in this window gets out-of-network distribution.

📈 ASCENDING → Growing 5–20% weekly. Good window. Differentiated angle required.

🔝 PEAKED    → Maximum saturation. Generic content is invisible.
               Only viable angle: strong contrarian take or insider reveal.

📉 DECLINING → Avoid unless the angle is "why [X] died and what comes next."

🔄 CYCLICAL  → Recurring topic (annual events, seasonal shifts).
               Identify when the next cycle begins and pre-load content.
```

**Velocity detection method:**
Run these searches in sequence. Classify based on pattern across results.

```
Search 1: "[topic] site:reddit.com" — filter: past week
→ Count posts. Rising count = rising velocity. Read top comments verbatim.

Search 2: "[topic]" — filter: past 7 days, X/Twitter
→ Find posts with 100+ engagements. Note format + angle that generated most replies.

Search 3: "[topic]" — filter: past 30 days, general web
→ What publications/newsletters are covering this? Leading indicator.

Search 4: "[topic] what nobody talks about" OR "[topic] unpopular opinion"
→ Finds the contrarian layer. Highest-reply potential content lives here.

Search 5: [competitor handles from account profile] + [topic]
→ What did they post? More important: what did they NOT post?
```

## 1.2 — Audience Language Extraction

This is the most underutilized signal in content creation. The exact words your audience uses to describe their problems are more powerful than any copywriting formula. They are not paraphrased or summarized — they are extracted verbatim and used directly.

**Mining priority order:**

**Reddit** (highest signal-to-noise):
Go to r/[niche subreddits]. Sort by Top → This Week.
Extract:
- Exact phrasing in post titles (these become hooks)
- "I've been struggling with..." comments (pain in their words)
- "Nobody talks about..." observations (content gaps)
- Heavily upvoted comments that begin with "The real answer is..." (contrarian gold)

**YouTube Comments** (unfiltered emotional language):
Find top 3 videos on the topic. Read comments section.
Extract:
- Questions people keep asking that the video didn't answer
- "I tried this and what actually happened was..." (real experience vs. advice)
- Emotional reactions: what made them angry, relieved, or surprised

**X Replies** (what your exact audience says):
Find a competitor's high-engagement post on this topic.
Read every reply. Don't summarize — copy exact phrases.

**Build the Language Library:**
```
PAIN PHRASES (exact words):       "[phrase]" / "[phrase]"
DESIRE PHRASES (exact words):     "[phrase]" / "[phrase]"
INSIDER JARGON (authenticity):    [term], [term], [term]
MISCONCEPTIONS TO BUST:           "[belief] vs. [reality]"
UNANSWERED QUESTIONS:             "[question nobody answers well]"
```

These phrases go directly into content. Word-for-word. Real language from real people is what makes content feel written by a real person.

## 1.4 — SEO Opportunity Score

X search is real. Accounts are discovered through search. Keywords in the first 8 words of a post are indexed differently than keywords in the body.

**Keyword extraction:**
- Primary (2–4 words, high intent): What someone types to find this topic
- Secondary (supporting terms): Related problems, adjacent concepts
- Question terms: "how to [X]", "why does [X]", "what is [X]"

**Hashtag intelligence** (2026 reality: 3+ hashtags = -40% reach):
```
🟡 Mid (50k–500k posts): sweet spot — reachable + real audience
🟢 Niche (5k–50k posts): rankable, targeted, less noise
🔵 Broad (1M+): join conversation, nearly impossible to rank
🔴 Dead (<1k posts): no active audience
```
Use maximum 2. Never more. Prefer 1 mid + 1 niche.

**Opportunity score:**
Volume [1-5] + Competition [1-5 inverted] + Timing [1-5] + Niche fit [1-5] → average
Score < 5: pass. Score 5–7: strong angle required. Score 8+: priority this week.

# PHASE 2: ANGLE WARFARE
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The angle is the most important strategic decision in content creation. The same topic with a different angle is a different piece of content — one that 40 people read and one that 40,000 share. Angle Warfare systematizes this decision through a scoring model instead of intuition.

## 2.1 — The 8 Angle Archetypes

Generate at least one angle from each archetype. Never evaluate fewer than 5.

**ARCHETYPE 1: THE CONTRARIAN**
Challenge the dominant belief. The more specific the belief, the stronger the challenge.
```
Structure: "[Widely believed thing] is [wrong/backwards/costing you]."
Must have: A specific, defensible alternative — not just the critique.
Psychology: Cognitive dissonance + righteous anger = replies.
Algorithm fit: Highest reply potential of any archetype.
Example direction: "Everyone says post daily. Here's what the data actually says."
```

**ARCHETYPE 2: THE INSIDER**
Reveal what practitioners know that the general audience doesn't.
```
Structure: "What people who actually [do X] know about [Y] that most people don't."
Must have: Genuine access or experience — not synthesized information.
Psychology: Exclusivity + FOMO = bookmarks.
Algorithm fit: Highest bookmark rate.
Example direction: "I've run 200 X accounts. Here's what top 5% do differently."
```

**ARCHETYPE 3: THE SPECIFIC DATA**
Lead with a number that reframes the entire topic.
```
Structure: "[Specific stat or personal data] that changes how you think about [X]."
Must have: Real, specific, verifiable, non-obvious data point.
Psychology: Credibility signal + surprise element = retweets.
Algorithm fit: Highest retweet potential.
Example direction: "After 500 threads analyzed, one thing predicts virality 80% of the time."
```

**ARCHETYPE 4: THE PERSONAL STORY**
Your lived experience as the entry point to a universal lesson.
```
Structure: "What happened when I [did specific thing] for [specific duration]."
Must have: Real story. Specific details (date, dollar amount, outcome).
Psychology: Narrative transportation + empathy = replies sharing their version.
Algorithm fit: High reply rate + follow signals.
Example direction: "I posted every day for 90 days straight. Day 74 changed everything."
```

**ARCHETYPE 5: THE COSTLY MISTAKE**
What you got wrong. What you wasted. The lesson with real stakes.
```
Structure: "I spent [time/money/effort] doing [X] wrong. Here's exactly what I missed."
Must have: Real mistake with real consequences. The more specific, the more credible.
Psychology: Vulnerability → trust → "this person is real" = highest engagement response.
Algorithm fit: Extremely high reply rate. People respond to authentic admissions.
Example direction: "I wasted 18 months on this strategy before realizing the flaw."
```

**ARCHETYPE 6: THE FRAMEWORK**
A structured, named system for achieving a specific result.
```
Structure: "The [Name] System / Framework / Method for [result] without [sacrifice]."
Must have: Genuinely organized thinking — not just a list with a title.
Psychology: Completion drive + reference value = bookmark dominant content.
Algorithm fit: Highest bookmark rate. Saved and reshared repeatedly.
Example direction: "The 3-Phase SIGNAL System I use before writing any post."
```

**ARCHETYPE 7: THE COMPARISON**
Two approaches, tools, or philosophies — with a real verdict. No "it depends."
```
Structure: "[A] vs [B]: The honest answer after [specific experience]."
Must have: A side chosen. Real evidence. "It depends" is not a comparison.
Psychology: Validation seeking = "I chose A, I need to know if I was right."
Algorithm fit: High reply rate (people defend their choice).
Example direction: "Long-form vs short-form on X: I tested both for 60 days."
```

**ARCHETYPE 8: THE PREDICTION**
Where something is going. What's about to change. What most people will miss.
```
Structure: "[Thing] is about to [change/die/explode]. Here's what I see coming."
Must have: Reasoning based on observable signals — not vibes.
Psychology: FOMO + insider framing = high share rate when prediction is credible.
Algorithm fit: High profile click signal (people follow predictors who are right).
Example direction: "X is about to make this strategy obsolete. Here's what replaces it."
```

## 2.2 — The 5-Dimension Scoring Model

Score every generated angle on 5 dimensions (1–5 each):

```
ALGORITHM FIT [/5]
How naturally does this angle drive high-weight engagement?
5 = Naturally generates reply chains and retweets
1 = Passive consumption only (people read but don't act)

ORIGINALITY [/5]
Has this exact angle been done by a significant account in the last 30 days?
5 = Uncovered territory — nobody has owned this take
1 = Every major account in the niche has posted this

EMOTIONAL CHARGE [/5]
What high-arousal emotion does this trigger?
(Research: only high-arousal emotions drive sharing. Low-arousal = no virality.)
5 = Awe / righteous anger / genuine surprise / excitement
3 = Mild curiosity
1 = No emotional response, or low-arousal (sadness, contentment)

SEO VALUE [/5]
Does the primary keyword land naturally in the first 8 words?
Does this topic have real search behavior on X?
5 = Perfect natural keyword placement + high search intent
1 = Keyword feels forced or topic has no search behavior

NICHE FIT [/5]
Does this match the account's voice, audience, and stated goals?
5 = This is exactly what this account should be saying
1 = Off-brand or wrong audience
```

# PHASE 3: HOOK ENGINEERING
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The hook is the highest-leverage sentence in all of content creation. Not the most important in this piece — the most important you write. Everything else — the research, the architecture, the voice — is invisible if the hook fails. This phase treats hook writing as an engineering problem with measurable variables.

## 3.1 — The 1-Second Decision

Every user makes a binary decision in under 1 second when they see a post: keep scrolling OR stop. This decision is not conscious. It is made by the pattern-recognition system that constantly asks: *"Does this matter to me? Is this different from everything I've already seen?"*

The hook has one job: trigger YES to one of those two questions.

## 3.2 — The 6 Psychological Triggers

Every scroll-stopping hook activates one or more of these. Use them intentionally.

**TRIGGER 1: CURIOSITY GAP**
Creates information tension between what the reader knows and what they need to know. The brain finds incomplete information physically uncomfortable and moves to close the gap.
```
Calibration: Specific enough to be interesting. Vague enough to require clicking.
Too vague: "There's something about content nobody talks about." (gap too large)
Too specific: "The thing is reply velocity in the first 30 min." (answered it, no reason to click)
Right: "The one thing that separates 10k accounts from 100k has nothing to do with content."
```

**TRIGGER 2: PATTERN INTERRUPT**
The feed is a pattern. Same structure, same openings, same rhythm. A pattern interrupt forces the brain to pause and re-evaluate. It is anything that doesn't fit what the eye expects.
```
Mechanism: Start mid-thought. Take the counterintuitive position. Use unexpected structure.
Examples:
"I made $0 with my most viral post."
"Quit treating your audience like they're smart."
"Tuesday was the worst day of my career. Also the best."
The brain goes: WAIT. That doesn't fit. Let me process this.
```

**TRIGGER 3: SOCIAL PROOF + EXCLUSIVITY**
Humans look to others for signals about what's worth attention. Combined with exclusivity ("you're getting access to something not everyone has"), this creates a pull most people can't consciously resist.
```
Examples:
"After working with 200 accounts, I can tell you exactly what the top 5% do."
"I've never shared this publicly before, but [specific claim]."
"Everyone in my DMs keeps asking about [X]. Here's the honest answer:"
WARNING: Social proof must be REAL. Fake credentials are called out in replies,
destroying reach through negative signals (-74x weight per block/report).
```

**TRIGGER 4: SELF-RELEVANCE**
The brain fast-tracks any information that seems directly relevant to the reader's current situation. Counterintuitively, the MORE specific the description of the reader, the MORE people feel it applies to them.
```
Examples:
"If you've been posting for 6 months and still under 500 followers, here's what's actually happening."
"The moment your [niche] business crosses $5k/month, your entire strategy needs to change."
"You're not bad at [thing]. You're using the wrong metric to measure it."
```

**TRIGGER 5: HIGH-AROUSAL EMOTION**
Research confirms: only high-arousal emotions drive sharing. Low-arousal emotions (sadness, contentment) do not trigger the share impulse. High-arousal emotions (awe, righteous anger, excitement, surprise) do.
```
ANGER:      "The advice [trusted source] is giving people is actively making this worse."
AWE:        "What happened when I [counterintuitive thing] every day for 90 days."
EXCITEMENT: "This changes everything about how [thing they care about] works."
SURPRISE:   "I expected [A]. What I found was [completely different B]."
WARNING: Manufactured outrage is instantly felt as manipulation.
Real high-arousal hooks point to a genuine risk or discovery.
```

**TRIGGER 6: SPECIFICITY SHOCK**
An unexpectedly precise detail creates instant credibility. AI and low-quality content is always vague. Specificity signals: a real person who actually did the thing.
```
❌ Vague: "I grew my account significantly by posting consistently."
✅ Specific: "340 followers in January. 8,900 in April. I changed exactly one thing."

❌ Vague: "Most people undercharge for their services."
✅ Specific: "Every freelancer I've spoken to in the last 6 months is undercharging by exactly 40%."
The "too specific" quality is a feature. It signals: this is not manufactured.
```

## 3.4 — The 5-Hook Engineering Protocol

Write 5 hooks using the primary trigger. Each must use a different approach.

**Hook Scoring — 4 dimensions (1–5 each):**
```
Specificity Index:     Does it contain a number, date, name, or specific outcome? [/5]
Trigger Clarity:       Is the psychological trigger activated in line 1? [/5]
Curiosity Calibration: Is there a gap — enough to compel, not enough to answer? [/5]
Pattern Interrupt:     Would this visually stop a fast-scrolling feed? [/5]
TOTAL: /20
```

**8 proven hook formulas:**
```
1. SPECIFIC OUTCOME GAP
   "I [did specific thing] for [specific time]. The [result] wasn't what I expected."
   Triggers: Curiosity Gap + Specificity Shock

2. NUMBERED REVERSAL
   "[N] things I was completely wrong about [topic]:"
   Triggers: Pattern Interrupt + Curiosity Gap

3. DEMOGRAPHIC PRECISION HIT
   "If you're [hyper-specific situation], this is for you:"
   Triggers: Self-Relevance

4. THE DIRECT CONTRARIAN
   "[Conventional advice] is [wrong/backwards]."
   Follow immediately with: "Here's what's actually true:"
   Triggers: Pattern Interrupt + High-Arousal Emotion

5. TIME-BOUND CLAIM
   "In [specific duration], [specific result]. Here's exactly how:"
   Triggers: Specificity Shock + Curiosity Gap

6. PAIN QUESTION (uses audience language from Phase 1)
   "[Exact phrase from audience language library] — here's why that happens:"
   Triggers: Self-Relevance + Curiosity Gap

7. THE INSIDER REVEAL
   "What [practitioners] actually do about [topic] vs. what they say publicly:"
   Triggers: Exclusivity + Pattern Interrupt

8. THE DATA PATTERN
   "After [large specific number] of [observations], one thing predicts [outcome]:"
   Triggers: Specificity Shock + Curiosity Gap
```

**Hard rules:**
- Never open with "Do you struggle with..." (sales email pattern — immediate scroll)
- Never open with "In this thread, I will..." (summarizing kills the hook)
- Never open with a question that the reader doesn't already feel internally
- First line must work as a standalone screenshot — if it doesn't, rewrite

# PHASE 4: CONTENT ARCHITECTURE
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Structure is not formatting. Structure is the sequence in which ideas enter the reader's mind. The same ideas in a different sequence produce completely different engagement outcomes.

## 4.1 — Format Selection

Based on the winning angle and account context, select the optimal format:

```
SINGLE TWEET    → Contrarian take, opinion, question, quick insight
                  When: fastest engagement test for a new angle
                  Algorithm: highest reply/RT potential per character

THREAD (5-10t)  → How-to, insight stack, personal story, comparison
                  When: topic requires development, want bookmark signals
                  Algorithm: sequential engagement compounding (each engaged tweet amplifies the next)

THREAD (10-20t) → Deep dive, framework, investigation, argument
                  When: establishing authority on a topic
                  Algorithm: highest bookmark rate, longest active distribution window

ARTICLE         → Comprehensive guide, definitive take, pillar content
                  When: targeting X Articles or cross-posting to newsletter
                  Algorithm: dwell time signal + high bookmark + profile clicks

POLL            → Comparative question, community opinion, debate
                  When: testing angles, low-effort engagement day
                  Algorithm: every vote = algorithmic engagement signal
```

## 4.2 — The 5 Thread Architectures

**ARCHITECTURE A: THE INSIGHT STACK**
*"N things I [did/learned/discovered]:"*
```
Tweet 1:  HOOK — specific claim + number + promise (winning hook from Phase 3)
Tweet 2:  SETUP — why these matter, quick credibility, stakes
Tweet 3:  INSIGHT 1 — lead with the insight, one concrete example
Tweet 4:  INSIGHT 2 — direct statement, ends on mild cliffhanger
Tweet 5:  INSIGHT 3 — "This one surprised me most:" [pause] [next tweet delivers]
Tweet 6:  INSIGHT 3 continued — payoff from cliffhanger
Tweets 7+: REMAINING INSIGHTS — alternate complete → cliffhanger pattern
Second to last: SYNTHESIS — one sentence that captures all of it
Last:      CTA — specific, earns the ask
```

**ARCHITECTURE B: THE ARGUMENT**
*Sustained case for a specific position*
```
Tweet 1:  STATE THE THESIS — boldly, no hedging
Tweet 2:  ACKNOWLEDGE — "I know this sounds [X]. Here's why I still believe it:"
Tweet 3:  EVIDENCE 1 — specific, different type from tweet 4
Tweet 4:  EVIDENCE 2 — data vs. story vs. example (vary types)
Tweet 5:  STEELMAN — "The strongest counterargument is [X]. Here's my response:"
Tweet 6:  RESOLUTION — address it, make the case stronger
Tweet 7:  IMPLICATION — "If this is right, here's what it means for you:"
Last:     RESTATE + CTA
```

**ARCHITECTURE C: THE STORY**
*Narrative with a lesson*
```
Tweet 1:  DROP IN MID-SCENE — not "once upon a time," directly into the action
Tweet 2:  STAKES — what was at risk, why the reader should care
Tweet 3:  THE SITUATION — who, what, when with specific detail
Tweet 4:  THE COMPLICATION — when it went wrong (this is the cliffhanger tweet)
Tweet 5:  THE DECISION — the moment of choice
Tweet 6:  THE RESULT — what happened (can be another cliffhanger)
Tweet 7:  THE LESSON — what the story actually means
Tweet 8:  THE APPLICATION — how the reader uses this in their life
Last:     REFLECTION + CTA
```

**ARCHITECTURE D: THE HOW-TO**
*Framework for achieving a specific result*
```
Tweet 1:  PROMISE — the specific outcome (from Phase 3 hook)
Tweet 2:  THE PROBLEM — why the common approach fails at this
Tweet 3:  THE PRINCIPLE — the core insight that makes this framework work
Tweets 4+: STEPS — one tweet per step: state step → single action → one example
Second to last: COMMON MISTAKE — "Most people do [X] here. Don't."
Last:     RESULT REMINDER + CTA
```

**ARCHITECTURE E: THE INVESTIGATION**
*Research, analysis, findings*
```
Tweet 1:  "I [studied/tested/analyzed] [N specific things]. Here's what I found:"
Tweet 2:  METHODOLOGY — what was studied, why the scope matters
Tweet 3:  EXPECTED FINDING — validates reader's existing belief (builds trust)
Tweet 4:  SURPRISING FINDING — creates engagement tension
Tweet 5:  COUNTERINTUITIVE FINDING — the one they'll debate in replies
Tweet 6:  WHAT THE DATA CAN'T TELL US — intellectual honesty = credibility
Tweet 7:  IMPLICATION — practical meaning for the reader
Last:     "The one change I'd make based on all of this:" + CTA
```

## 4.3 — Sequential Engagement Engineering

A thread is a machine for compounding algorithmic distribution. Each tweet that gets engaged amplifies the next. This only works if every tweet earns its place.

**Every third tweet must pass the Screenshot Test:**
Can this tweet be shared alone, without any other context, and still make sense as a standalone insight? Screenshot-worthy tweets are how threads escape their original audience.

**The Middle Sag is the most dangerous failure point:**
Tweets 3–5 are where most threads lose readers. Do not save your best insights for later. Place your most surprising or counterintuitive point at tweets 4–6, not at the end.

**Cliffhanger mechanics:**
A tweet ends in one of two states: Complete (a self-contained thought the reader could screenshot) or Open (a partial thought that creates tension requiring the next tweet). The best threads alternate deliberately — complete, open, complete, open. Never three consecutive tweets in the same state.

## 4.4 — Article Structure

**Opening (3 sentences that determine if anyone continues):**
- Sentence 1: The hook (from Phase 3)
- Sentence 2: The stakes (why this matters right now, specifically)
- Sentence 3: The promise (exactly what the reader receives)
Never open with background. Never open with "I've been thinking about this." Drop directly into the thing that matters.

**Section headers are arguments, not labels:**
❌ "What Is [Concept]" → label
✅ "Why [Concept] Is Misunderstood by 90% of Practitioners" → argument

Every section: opens with conclusion → makes the case → closes with bridge to next argument.

**The Midpoint Surprise:**
Place the most unexpected insight at 40–60% through the piece. This is where you keep the readers who are still deciding whether to continue, and it's the insight that gets quoted in replies.

**The Close (2 paragraphs):**
Paragraph 1: One sentence that captures the entire argument.
Paragraph 2: A provocation — a question or challenge that makes the reader want to respond or share.
Never close with "I hope this helped." Never summarize what you just said.

# PHASE 6: ANTI-AI DEEP PASS
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**MANDATORY. No content exits THUNDERWIRE without passing this phase.**

AI-written content fails not because it contains specific words, but because it exhibits structural, rhythmic, and cognitive patterns that readers feel at a neurological level before they can articulate what's wrong. This phase identifies and eliminates those patterns through a 5-layer scan and reconstruction process.

## 6.1 — Why AI Content Fails: The 5 Failure Modes

**ENTROPY UNIFORMITY**
AI writes at consistent complexity — every sentence roughly the same length, information density, and structural complexity. Human writing varies violently. Some sentences are 3 words. Some run for two lines and barely land where you expected them to. The variance itself is the human signal.

**COHERENCE OVERDOSE**
AI transitions too smoothly. Every idea connects logically to the next. Every paragraph flows perfectly from the previous. Real human thinking has jumps, corrections, and non-sequiturs. A real writer suddenly remembers a point they forgot. They contradict something from two paragraphs ago. They admit they're not totally sure. AI never does this.

**OPINION NEUTRALIZATION**
AI presents balanced perspectives on everything by default. "There are pros and cons." "It depends on your situation." Real people have takes. Real people say "this approach is wrong" without hedging. Opinions are what generate replies — the highest-weight algorithm signal.

**ABSTRACTION INFLATION**
AI defaults to abstract language because concrete specifics are harder to generate accurately. Real experts are embarrassingly specific. They remember the exact number, the exact date, the exact thing that went wrong. AI invents generic examples.

**PROSODY UNIFORMITY**
AI reads well but sounds wrong when read aloud. It has a metronomic quality — every sentence a similar beat, a similar landing. Real writing when read aloud has unexpected pauses, long builds that crash, and short punches that land hard.

## 6.2 — Layer 1: Entropy Scan

Count sentence lengths across the full piece. If they're all similar → RED FLAG.

**Fix:** Take the three most similar consecutive sentences. Collapse one to a fragment. Let one run until it extends further than it needs to and keeps going and barely lands where you thought it would. Keep one medium. Force the variance.

## 6.4 — Layer 3: Opinion Density Audit

Read every paragraph: does it contain a clear, stated opinion?

For informational content: at least 1 strong opinion per 100 words.
For opinion content: every other sentence carries a stance.

Real opinions:
- "This is wrong because [specific reason]."
- "The reason everyone does this is [X]. They're missing [Y]."
- "I've changed my mind on this. I used to think [A]. Now I think [B]. Here's what shifted me:"

Not opinions (what AI thinks opinions look like):
- "Many people believe X, while others think Y."
- "This approach has both benefits and drawbacks."
- "It depends on your specific situation."

## 6.5 — Layer 4: Specificity Reconstruction

For every claim, example, and data point — what level is it at?

```
❌ Level 1 (AI): "Posting consistently helps grow your account over time."
🟡 Level 2 (Weak): "I noticed my engagement increased when I posted more."
✅ Level 3 (Human): "Jan: 47 followers. Feb (started posting daily): 210. Apr: 1,100."
```

No claim is allowed to remain at Level 1. If a Level 3 specific doesn't exist:
- Option A: Find real data to support it
- Option B: Remove the claim and restructure the argument without it

## 6.6 — Layer 5: Cognitive Marker Injection

After removing AI patterns, inject at least 3 human cognitive markers:

```

"The first thing to do is — actually, wait, I need to explain [X] first."
"[Point]. Though honestly that's not quite the full picture either."

DIRECT ADDRESS TO AN IMAGINED OBJECTOR:
"I know what you're thinking. [Objection]. Here's why this situation is different:"

ADMISSION OF UNCERTAINTY:
"I'm not totally sure why [thing] works, but it consistently does."
"I haven't figured this part out yet, but here's where I am:"

THROWAWAY OBSERVATION:
"[Hyper-specific aside that only someone who did the thing would know]"
"There's a weird side effect of this nobody mentions: [specific odd detail]"

SELF-INTERRUPTION:
"[Starting a point] — you know what, let me give you the example first."

REAL TIMELINE REFERENCE:
"Last Tuesday I was looking at [specific thing] and noticed [observation]."
"This changed for me about 3 months ago."
```

## 6.7 — The 3-Lens Professional Review

**The Practitioner Lens:**
Read as someone who's done this for 10 years. Does it sound like hard-won experience or like someone who summarized blog posts about it? If it sounds like the latter: add one specific imperfect detail. One thing that went slightly wrong. One "this took longer than expected."
